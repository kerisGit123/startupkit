### **Redesigned Manga Production Structure**

We’ll keep it streamlined but powerful, with key elements tied together in a way that makes it easy to manage.

---

#### **1. Universe Manager (World-Building)**

This is where the foundation of your world is established. Think of it as the "reference" area where everything that’s important to your manga world lives.

**Key components**:

* **Rules**: Power systems, magic, technologies, laws of nature.
* **Locations**: Cities, towns, important landmarks.
* **Character Database**: Centralized character information (appearance, traits, relationships, growth).

---

#### **2. Arc Tagging System**

Instead of breaking up the structure into "arcs" as separate production units, **arcs are simply tags** that are applied to **episodes**.

**Key components**:

* **Arc Tags**:

  * Example: “Training Arc”, “Conflict Arc”, “Climax Arc”, “Resolution Arc”.
  * Each **episode** can be tagged with one or more arcs as needed.
* **Arc Timeline**:

  * This can help you map out how arcs unfold across episodes, and what needs to happen in each one to progress the arc.

---

#### **3. Episode Planning (The Chapter Level)**

Each **Episode** is a distinct, manageable unit of your manga—typically a chapter or a major story beat. It’s where the story happens in real-time.

**Key components**:

* **Episode Title**: The name of the episode or chapter.
* **Arc Tag(s)**: This episode is tagged with the arc(s) it belongs to (e.g., Training Arc).
* **Summary**: A brief description of the episode’s plot.
* **Characters**: A list of the key characters appearing in the episode.
* **Key Moments**: High-level beats (e.g., major battle, emotional moment).
* **Notes**: Space for any quick thoughts, references, or ideas.

---

#### **4. Page Breakdown (The Layout Unit)**

Each **episode** is broken down into individual **pages**. This is where the visual storytelling starts to take shape.

**Key components**:

* **Page Number**: The specific page number in the episode.
* **Summary**: A brief description of what happens on the page (e.g., “Hero faces off with the villain”).
* **Panel Count**: Number of panels on the page.
* **Character(s)**: Which characters appear in this page.
* **Key Scenes/Visual Elements**: What key visual moments need to be included in this page (e.g., “Close-up of character’s face,” “Hero running through a city”).

---

#### **5. Panel Breakdown (The Visual Unit)**

Each **page** is divided into **panels**, the core building block of manga storytelling. This is where you plan your compositions, camera angles, character placements, and more.

**Key components**:

* **Panel Number**: Numbered panels to keep track of panel order.
* **Description**: What happens in the panel (e.g., “Wide shot of the battlefield,” “Close-up of a character’s emotional reaction”).
* **Character(s)**: Which character(s) are featured in the panel.
* **Visual Style**: Notes on artistic choices (e.g., “Speed lines for action,” “Shaded panel for suspense”).
* **Dialogue/SFX**: What dialogue is present, and what sound effects are needed.

### **Advantages of This Redesign**

1. **Simple Yet Structured**: The **Episode → Page → Panel** structure keeps things clear and easy to follow. With arcs as tags, it reduces complexity while giving you full control over large plot movements.

2. **Flexible and Scalable**: Adding or adjusting arcs as tags doesn't require a major rework of your system. This structure grows as your story develops. You can keep new episodes organized without worrying about the overall arc structure in the beginning.

3. **Easy Navigation**: Since **arcs** are **tags**, you can quickly filter episodes and pages by their related arcs, which is great for long-term planning and referencing. You don’t have to constantly manage arcs at the production level.

4. **Clear Visual Planning**: With a page and panel breakdown, your creative process stays focused. Each panel is a manageable piece of the puzzle, and each page reflects how the plot moves forward. This gives you a clear roadmap for your art and storytelling.

5. **Consistency**: The **Character Database** and **Universe Manager** keep all essential details in one place, which is key for consistency in the long run. You can ensure characters don’t lose traits, locations stay true, and arcs progress logically.
