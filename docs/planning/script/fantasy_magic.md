# Fantasy / Magic & VFX Scene Prompts

## When to use this category
Sorcery, spellcasting, energy beams, cursed auras, magical transformations, elemental powers, supernatural movement. The VFX IS the character — their power is visible as light, smoke, particles, or energy coiling around their body.

## Default model
🔴 **Seedance 2.0** — always. Magic scenes require complex particle systems, energy compositing, and light interaction that 2.0 handles and 1.5 Pro cannot.

---

## Language patterns observed in real prompts

From analyzing creator prompts, the most effective magic scene writing uses these specific patterns:

**Energy description language:**
- "coiling around her ankles like liquid fire" — give energy a physical behaviour (coil, pulse, crackle, radiate, seep)
- "orange light swirling violently around her torso" — direction + intensity + body part
- "blinding orb of concentrated solar energy manifesting in her hand" — name the energy type, describe its birth
- "black cursed smoke leaking from his body" — leaking implies involuntary, atmospheric
- "shadow energy constantly flowing" — "constantly" establishes it as ambient, not a burst

**Motion build structure (seen across prompts):**
1. **Arrival / Rest** — character still, energy beginning (coiling, wisping)
2. **Intensity Build** — energy escalates, environment reacts (fabric flutters, stones float)
3. **Release / Attack** — energy fires, the physical consequence is shown

**Camera language for magic:**
- "panning up" on a reveal — bottom to top builds scale and dread
- "lens flare" at energy output point — confirms the light source is real in-world
- "volumetric lighting" for beam shots — the beam has physical mass in the air
- "casting long shadows across the arena" — magic light creates practical shadows

## Image Prompt Guide for Fantasy / Magic

The image prompt must establish: where the energy originates on the body, what colour and physical texture it has, what the environment looks like being affected by it, and the camera angle that serves the power.

**Energy colour = character identity.** Orange solar fire ≠ blue frost ≠ green poison. Always name it with two descriptors: colour + physical metaphor.

Template:
```
[Character description: clothing, body position, expression]. [Energy origin point on body + energy behaviour: coiling, pulsing, radiating, leaking]. [Energy colour and physical texture: "liquid fire", "crackling violet arcs", "black smoke seeping"]. [Environment reaction: floating debris, scorched ground, fabric moving in supernatural wind]. [Lighting source: is the energy the light source? Name it.]. [Camera angle + framing]. [Atmospheric: embers, dust, smoke, particle density]. Cinematic, photorealistic / [style], 16:9.
```

Example:
```
A sorceress in red and gold robes standing inside an ancient stone ritual circle, both arms raised, orange solar energy coiling violently around her torso and arms like liquid fire — the energy is the only light source, casting warm amber across the stone. Floating stone fragments orbit her body at mid-height, fabric snapping in a supernatural outward wind. Close-up, slight low angle — she looks down at her own power. Embers drift upward from the ground. Dark forest ruins in soft bokeh behind. Cinematic, photorealistic, 16:9.
```

## Video Prompt Guide for Fantasy / Magic (2.0)

Magic scenes need a **three-beat power arc** and explicit description of how the energy moves over time — it must escalate, not stay constant.

**Power arc:**
1. **Ignition beat** — energy first appears. Small, controlled, contained.
2. **Escalation beat** — energy grows. Environment begins to react. Debris lifts. Wind picks up.
3. **Release beat** — energy fires or explodes outward. Camera captures the consequence.

**Supernatural movement language:**
- "teleportation effects" — character disappears and reappears; describe which direction
- "shadow afterimages" — trails of the previous position remain briefly visible
- "instantaneous" — use this word to imply impossible speed
- "smooth-motion trails" — for sword or strike paths through energy

**For beam / projectile shots:**
- Always describe the beam's width, colour, and how it interacts with the air it passes through
- "volumetric lighting" — the beam has god-ray quality, visible in atmosphere
- "scorched marks on the stone" — show the aftermath, not just the beam

Format (Seedance 2.0):
```
Shot on ALEXA 65mm anamorphic lens. Photorealistic cinematic quality. Aspect ratio 16:9 widescreen. [Colour grade matching the energy palette]. Film grain. [Environment: ruins, arena, shrine, city — the setting that makes the magic legible]. [Particle atmosphere: embers, smoke, dust, debris density]. [Energy ambient light: what colour does the magic cast on surfaces?]. Dolby Vision HDR.

0.0s–Xs: [Character position]. [Energy state: first appearance, contained]. [Camera: establishes the character and the environment in relationship].

X.Xs–Xs: [Escalation]. [Energy grows — describe direction and speed of growth]. [Environment reaction: what floats, what cracks, what moves]. [Camera pushes in or pans up with the energy].

X.Xs–Xs: [RELEASE]. [Energy fires or explodes — direction, target, consequence]. [TIME REMAP if the release is the hero moment]. [VFX effect NAME in CAPS: ENERGY BEAM, SHOCKWAVE RING, PARTICLE BURST]. → [TRANSITION TYPE].
```

## Signature Fantasy / Magic Techniques

- **The Power Build Pan** — camera starts low on the feet where energy first appears, pans slowly up the body as energy escalates, arrives at the face exactly at peak power. The pan IS the build.
- **Floating Debris Orbit** — stone fragments, leaves, or objects circle the character at mid-height. Implies gravitational disruption. Each orbit slightly faster than the last.
- **Energy Lens Flare Sync** — the magic energy source creates an anamorphic lens flare. Every pulse of energy = a pulse in the flare. Links the camera to the magic.
- **The Aftermath Hold** — after the energy fires, camera holds on the scorched/cracked result while smoke settles. The silence after the power.
- **Shadow Afterimage Trail** — supernatural speed shown by leaving 2–3 translucent copies of the character's previous positions. They fade within one second.
- **Supernatural Wind Tells** — fabric, hair, and environmental debris react to magical energy before it's visible. The wind comes first, then the light.
