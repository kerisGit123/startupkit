# Sci-Fi / Mech & Transformation Scene Prompts

## When to use this category
Robotic transformations, mechanical suit assembly, giant robots, spider mechs, powered armour, technological metamorphosis, vehicle-to-robot conversion. The defining feature is **mechanical parts visibly reconfiguring** — the transformation itself is the VFX event.

## Default model
🔴 **Seedance 2.0** — always. Mech and transformation scenes require complex part-by-part animation, hard surface VFX, and large-scale subject handling.

---

## Language patterns observed in real prompts

**Transformation language — the specific words that work:**
- "mechanical parts shift" — use "shift" as the pre-transformation verb; it implies internal rearrangement
- "hydraulic pistons suddenly deploying from the chassis" — name the mechanical sub-components (pistons, hydraulics, actuators, armour plating, articulated joints)
- "armored segments pivot outward with mechanical precision and reconfigure into a barrel-shaped cannon" — "pivot", "reconfigure", "segments" are the core verbs
- "multi-jointed articulated robotic legs burst from the white truck's body" — "burst from" implies violent emergence
- "exposed engine parts and spinning gears" — show the inside of the machine during transformation
- "forearm armor plating splits along gold seams" — specify the seam line where the armour opens

**Scale language:**
- "colossal mechanical spider body" — scale adjective first, then type
- "giant legs scuttling across the lanes with predatory speed" — animal movement vocabulary applied to mechanical subjects creates menace
- "legs scuttling with insect-like precision leaving deep gouges in the asphalt" — environmental damage communicates weight and scale
- "sun glinting off chrome surfaces" — reflective surface detail signals photoreal quality

**Energy / weapon language:**
- "amber coils spin up inside the barrel" — coils spinning up is the power-charge visual
- "amber plasma condenses at the muzzle" — "condenses" is the key word for energy gathering at a weapon tip
- "thick, amber-orange beam erupts from the cannon, cutting across the stadium in a perfectly straight line" — beam described as thick + colour + behaviour

**Camera language for mech:**
- "frontal low-angle shot" — looking up at the mech establishes its size over the viewer
- "wide tracking shot pulling back rapidly" — camera retreating from the mech as it fires implies the operator's instinct to flee
- "close-up push-in on the cannon barrel" — the weapon gets its own close-up before firing
- "shadows stretching across the road, epic scale" — shadows communicate size when the subject itself doesn't fit the frame

## Image Prompt Guide for Sci-Fi / Mech

Mech image prompts must establish **three scale references simultaneously**: the mech itself, something human-scale for size comparison, and the environment being affected by the mech's presence.

**Transformation moment taxonomy:**
- **Pre-transform** — vehicle/person in original form. Faint trembling or part-shift hints visible.
- **Mid-transform** — parts actively separating, inner mechanics exposed, shape caught between two states.
- **Post-transform** — final form fully emerged. Maximum menace. Environment showing the impact.

Template:
```
[Subject in transformation state: pre / mid / post]. [Mechanical details visible: name specific parts — pistons, actuators, armour segments, cockpit, limbs]. [Scale reference: what human-scale object is in frame for comparison?]. [Environmental impact: what does the ground/road/arena look like under the mech's weight?]. [Surface materials: chrome, matte black, weathered white, brushed steel — name them]. [Lighting: what light hits the metal surfaces — sun glint, explosion-lit, city ambient]. [Camera angle: low angle + distance to establish scale]. Cinematic, photorealistic, 16:9.
```

Example:
```
A semi-truck mid-transformation — the cab has risen fully off the ground, suspended between states. The undercarriage has split open, four multi-jointed mechanical legs fully deployed and planted on the highway asphalt, crushing lane markings beneath each footfall. The inner chassis is exposed: spinning gears, hydraulic cylinders, structural ribs visible inside the cavity. Scale reference: a small sedan is visible in the adjacent lane, dwarfed. Chrome leg joints glint in direct desert sun, weathered white paint still visible on the upper cab panels. Extreme low angle, camera at asphalt level. Deep shadows stretch from the legs across the road. Cinematic, photorealistic, 16:9.
```

## Video Prompt Guide for Sci-Fi / Mech (2.0)

Mech video prompts need a **part-by-part transformation sequence** — the viewer should see each mechanical element emerge or reconfigure in order. This is a directorial choice: establish → begin → escalate → complete.

**Transformation beat structure:**
1. **Tremor beat** — subject shakes or vibrates. Something is happening inside.
2. **First emergence beat** — the first mechanical part breaks through the surface. Sparks and material fly.
3. **Escalation beat** — multiple parts deploy simultaneously. The original form is losing coherence.
4. **Complete form beat** — transformation finished. The mech stands fully assembled. Hold on the complete form.

**Mech movement language:**
- "scuttling with insect-like precision" — insect vocabulary for multi-legged mechs
- "predatory speed" — implies the mech has agency and intention
- "weaving between lanes" — spatial description, the mech understands its environment
- "leaving deep gouges in the asphalt" — weight consequence, every footfall has cost

**Weapon charge sequence:**
1. Coils begin spinning (visual: rotation + emitted light)
2. Energy condenses at the muzzle (visual: the tip glows, heat shimmer around it)
3. Operator locks stance (character prepares for recoil)
4. Beam erupts (describe width, colour, atmospheric interaction)
5. Impact explosion (beam hits target — scale of destruction)

Format (Seedance 2.0):
```
Shot on ALEXA 65mm anamorphic lens. Photorealistic cinematic quality. Aspect ratio 16:9 widescreen. [Colour grade: desert heat = warm amber / cyberpunk night = teal-neon / industrial = desaturated steel]. Film grain. [Environment: highway, arena, port, city — describe scale indicators]. [Atmospheric: dust, sparks, exhaust, debris, heat shimmer on metal surfaces]. [Scale reference in frame at all times]. Dolby Vision HDR.

0.0s–Xs: [Pre-transform — original form established]. [Camera: wide, low angle, scale reference visible]. [First tremor or mechanical shift noted].

X.Xs–Xs: [Part emergence]. [NAME the parts deploying: legs, armour, cannon, cockpit]. [Direction of emergence: down, outward, forward]. [Sparks and material debris from the breach points].

X.Xs–Xs: [Escalation — multiple parts simultaneously]. [Environmental damage visible: gouges, cracks, displaced vehicles]. [Camera pulls back as the subject grows].

X.Xs–Xs: [Complete form revealed]. [TIME REMAP if the final reveal is the hero moment]. [Hold on the complete mech against the environment]. → [TRANSITION TYPE — often → HARD CUT to the attack beat].
```

## Signature Sci-Fi / Mech Techniques

- **The Inside-Out Transform** — camera is positioned close enough to see the interior mechanics as the outer shell splits. Gears, pistons, and structure briefly exposed before the new form closes around them.
- **The Asphalt Weight Test** — as the mech's weight transfers to the ground, the asphalt cracks and crazes outward from each foot contact. Shot from below, at ground level, looking up through the cracks.
- **The Weapon Charge Close-Up** — dedicated scene for weapon power-up. Start on the barrel at rest, push in as coils begin spinning, energy condenses at the tip. Cut when the muzzle reaches maximum brightness.
- **The Scale Creep** — open on a wide shot where the mech appears small in frame. Over 3–4 seconds of tracking, the mech grows to fill the frame. The viewer gradually realises the scale.
- **The Predatory Approach** — low-angle frontal, mech walking directly toward the camera. Camera holds. The mech grows until it nearly fills the frame, then cuts.
- **Debris POV** — camera is at the level of objects being displaced by the mech: crushed cars, thrown debris, cracked concrete. The human experience of the mech's scale.
