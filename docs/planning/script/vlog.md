# Vlog Scene Prompts

## When to use this category
Handheld casual footage, talking-to-camera moments, travel documentation, lifestyle content, behind-the-scenes, day-in-the-life sequences. The feel is authentic, slightly imperfect, human. Contrast to cinematic polish — vlog intentionally avoids it.

## Default model
🟢 **Seedance 1.5 Pro** — vlog is almost always 1.5 Pro. The handheld, documentary aesthetic is a 1.5 Pro strength.

---

## Image Prompt Guide for Vlogs

Vlog image prompts lean into **intentional imperfection**: natural available light, handheld framing that's slightly off-centre, real environments with visible clutter or activity. Fight the instinct to make it too perfect.

Key signals of vlog aesthetic:
- Natural light only (windows, outdoor, streetlight) — avoid professional lighting rigs
- Slightly asymmetric framing — subject at 60% not perfectly centred
- Visible environment activity in background — people, movement, chaos
- Casual clothing, authentic expression — not posed, mid-action or mid-sentence
- Lens choices: wide (17–24mm feel) for immersion; or "selfie" point-of-view framing

Template:
```
[Subject — casual description, clothing that fits the environment]. [Activity: what are they doing, mid-action preferred]. [Location + time of day]. [Light source: window, overcast outdoor, neon shopfront, golden hour]. [Framing: handheld feel, slightly off-centre, subject doesn't fill the whole frame]. [Background activity]. Shot on a mirrorless camera with natural light, handheld, 16:9.
```

Example:
```
A young man in a cream hoodie, mid-laugh, holding a street food skewer at arm's length toward the camera. Night market setting — lanterns strung overhead, food stalls packed behind him, crowd movement blurred. Orange-warm light from food stall flames and pendant bulbs. Handheld POV framing, slightly tilted, he fills about 60% of the left side of frame. Shot on a mirrorless camera with natural light, handheld, 16:9.
```

## Video Prompt Guide for Vlogs (1.5 Pro)

Vlog video prompts are about **movement that feels unplanned** — the camera reacts to the subject rather than leading it. The operator is following, not directing.

Key camera behaviours:
- **Handheld drift** — small, organic, unmotivated camera sway. Not a shake — a sway.
- **Reactive pan** — camera pans to follow subject movement a half-beat late (as if the operator had to react)
- **Environmental wander** — camera drifts off-subject momentarily to capture something in the environment, then returns
- **Selfie hold** — subject holds the camera (POV framing), arm visible, slight instability

Subject behaviour to write:
- Mid-sentence or mid-action starts — don't begin on a composed posed moment
- Natural eye breaks — subject looks away from camera then back
- Unscripted gestures — adjusting a strap, pointing at something, laughing without cue

Format:
```
0.0s–Xs: [Handheld camera starting position]. [Subject activity — not a posed start]. [Camera instability level: subtle drift, moderate reactive, loose and active]. [What's happening in background].

X.Xs–Xs: [Subject does something — camera reacts]. [Pan direction + speed]. [Environmental detail that enters the frame].

X.Xs–Xs: [Key vlog beat: laughing, pointing at something, addressing camera directly]. [Camera response]. → [CUT or → NEXT CLIP feel].
```

## Signature Vlog Techniques

- **The Accidental Beautiful Shot** — camera wanders from the subject onto an incidentally beautiful detail (light through leaves, reflection in water, a stranger's face) then cuts back. Feels discovered, not staged.
- **The Turn-Around Reveal** — subject walking away from camera turns to face it suddenly. Camera had to spin to follow, slight over-rotation.
- **The Hand-Off POV** — camera passed from one person to another mid-shot. Visible instability during the transfer, then the new holder steadies.
- **The Environmental Pan-Off** — instead of cutting on a person, pan off them and onto the environment (skyline, crowd, sign) as a transition. Camera lingers on the detail before the cut.
- **The Laugh Break** — subject breaks character, genuinely laughs, camera catches it uncut. The moment of unguarded authenticity is left in.
- **Speed Ramp Transit** — walking or travel footage at normal speed with a brief TIME RAMP to hyperspeed through a transition (getting on train, crossing the street), then back to normal. Common vlog pacing trick.
