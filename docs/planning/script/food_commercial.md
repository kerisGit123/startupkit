# Food & Product Commercial Scene Prompts

## When to use this category
Food preparation, ingredient reveals, product showcases, restaurant/chef content, macro food shots, brand ad sequences. The subject is something being consumed, presented, or sold. Every shot must make the viewer want it.

## Default model
🔴 **Seedance 2.0** for hero shots with complex physics (cheese stretch, ingredient assembly, grease flare-up, floating ingredients).  
🟢 **Seedance 1.5 Pro** for simple preparation shots, static chef scenes, clean close-up cooking without VFX.

---

## Language patterns observed in real prompts

Creators writing food/commercial prompts use a distinct vocabulary that activates appetite and desire. These patterns appeared consistently:

**Texture and material language:**
- "juicy, red interior with visible marbling and a glistening, seared crust" — three texture descriptors minimum
- "thick cheddar slices... cheese instantly bubbles and drapes in slow motion" — the slow-motion + material physics combo
- "extreme macro on cheese stretch and viscosity" — "viscosity" is the key word for cheese/liquid shots
- "heat shimmer distortion above the patty" — heat shimmer appears in almost every premium food prompt
- "sesame seeds and droplets airborne" — impact physics applied to food
- "masala particles" / "coarse salt crystals" — specific ingredient names, not generic "seasoning"

**Lighting language for food:**
- "backlit" — liquid and translucent ingredients (juice explosions, water spray) need backlight to catch the light
- "chiaroscuro lighting" — high-contrast dramatic food photography style
- "warm amber highlights" for cooked/warm food; "cold cinematic grade" for raw/frozen food
- "rim light" catching the edge of ingredients — cobalt-blue rim light on the chef from the example
- "dramatic food lighting" — this exact phrase signals the stock-photo premium look

**Camera language:**
- "camera slowly pushing in, focus locked on pizza" — the slow push-in on hero food is standard
- "smooth circular camera orbit" — the 360° orbit is the signature hero food shot finisher
- "macro shot" / "extreme macro" — food prompts go closer than any other category
- "4K/8K quality" — creators frequently name resolution in commercial prompts
- "premium ad look" / "premium commercial finish" — these exact phrases appear as style anchors

**Action language:**
- "flying pieces, clean cuts, juice splashes" — three consequences of one action
- "dough tossed in air, quick 360° camera rotation, oven fire burst" — action + camera reaction + environment reaction in one beat
- "ingredients fly into a vertical column mid-air" — the vertical float assembly is a signature commercial move
- "360-degree orbit around the hovering assembly in bullet-time" — bullet-time for food assembly is a real technique used

## Image Prompt Guide for Food & Commercial

Every food image prompt needs: the ingredient/product at its most appetising moment, the exact texture description, the lighting that makes it look premium, and the camera distance.

**Food shot types:**
- **Hero / Glamour shot** — full product, best angle, maximum appetite appeal. The final frame.
- **Macro / Texture shot** — extreme close-up on a single surface: cheese stretch, meat grain, liquid bead.
- **Action / Process shot** — mid-action: knife through tomato, patty hitting grill, cheese being lifted.
- **Chef / Handler shot** — human relationship to the food: pride, precision, presentation.
- **Environmental / Atmosphere shot** — the kitchen, the setting, the context that makes the food believable.

Template (hero shot):
```
[Food/product name + specific preparation state: seared, raw, assembled, mid-cook]. [Texture description: three specific material details]. [Lighting: source + quality + what it does to the surface]. [Camera: distance + angle — macro, close-up, three-quarter, top-down]. [Background: clean dark, kitchen environment, wet slate, wooden board]. [Atmospheric: steam, smoke, heat shimmer, water droplets, airborne particles]. Cinematic, photorealistic, 16:9. [Resolution note if commercial: 4K quality].
```

Example (macro):
```
Extreme macro close-up of thick cheddar cheese draped over a seared beef patty, mid-melt — the cheese has begun to bubble and flow downward but hasn't reached the bun. The surface texture shows individual bubble pockets forming, high gloss where fat is rendering, matte where cheese is still cooling. Backlit from below by charcoal grill heat, warm amber glow catching the cheese edges. Camera dead-front, extremely tight. Heat shimmer distortion visible in the air above. Dark background, no environment detail. Cinematic, photorealistic, 4K quality.
```

## Video Prompt Guide for Food & Commercial (2.0)

Commercial food prompts are structured as **a sequence of appetite moments** — each shot builds desire through a different sense: texture, heat, assembly, presentation. The arc moves from raw → process → hero.

**Standard commercial arc:**
1. **Ingredient intro** — show the raw material at its best
2. **Process moments** — cooking, cutting, assembling — the transformation
3. **Hero reveal** — the finished product, best angle, maximum desire
4. **Optional: Handler** — chef presents, hands lift, eyes react

**Physics language for food VFX:**
- "juice explosion backlit" — tomato/citrus cut in slow-mo, juice bursts radially
- "grease-fire flare-up engulfs the frame" — patty hitting grill causes a fire burst
- "visible cheese stretch and viscosity" — cheese being lifted from the melt, stretching
- "ingredients fly into a vertical column mid-air" — gravity-defying assembly shot
- "impact sends sesame seeds and droplets airborne" — burger landing on board
- "water droplet spray catching the rim light" — fresh produce pulled from water

Format (Seedance 2.0):
```
Shot on ALEXA 65mm anamorphic lens. Photorealistic cinematic quality. Aspect ratio 16:9 widescreen. [Colour grade: warm amber for cooked food / cold clinical for raw/frozen]. Film grain. [Kitchen environment: grill, prep surface, kitchen type]. [Atmospheric: steam density, smoke, heat shimmer, ember count if charcoal]. [Lighting rig: backlight direction, rim light colour, key light quality]. Dolby Vision HDR.

0.0s–Xs: [Ingredient/action intro]. [Camera angle and distance]. [What the food does physically — describe the physics].

X.Xs–Xs: [Process beat]. [TIME REMAP for hero action: knife through tomato, cheese drop, dough toss]. [What flies, splashes, or stretches — specific physics].

X.Xs–Xs: [Hero reveal / assembly]. [Camera: orbit or push-in]. [Final product described at its most appetising]. → [END or → SLOW FADE].
```

## Signature Food & Commercial Techniques

- **The Vertical Float Assembly** — ingredients hover in a vertical column and assemble from top to bottom in slow-motion. 360° camera orbit around the hovering stack. Classic premium burger/sandwich shot.
- **The Juice Explosion Backlit** — extreme slow-mo knife strike on a tomato or citrus. Juice erupts radially, backlit so every droplet catches the light. TIME REMAP at the moment of contact.
- **The Cheese Stretch Hold** — camera locked on cheese being lifted. The stretch is the shot. Let it go as long as physically possible before cutting.
- **The Grill Fire Burst** — raw protein hits a white-hot grill surface, triggering a grease-fire flare-up that momentarily engulfs the lower frame. TIME REMAP at the burst.
- **The Hero Landing Impact** — finished product dropped or placed onto a surface, the impact sends sesame seeds, droplets, or crumbs airborne. Camera at side level. Micro-physics sell the weight.
- **The Circular Orbit Reveal** — camera does one slow 360° orbit around the finished dish on a surface. The orbit IS the hero shot — it shows every angle and lets the product fill the frame completely.
- **Heat Shimmer Push** — camera slowly pushes in toward a hot food subject, focus locked, while heat shimmer creates a gentle distortion at the edges of the frame. The push and shimmer together signal extreme heat.
